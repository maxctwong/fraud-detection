Fraud Detection on Credit Card Transactions

Why Fraud Detection Matters?

  • Digital payment systems increase vulnerability to cyber threats
  • Fraud affects billions globally
  • Companies need better fraud detection tools
  • Machine learning offers a scalable, data-driven solution

How do transactional and behavioral factors affect the likelihood of a person being a fraud victim?

  1. RQ1: Would certain types of merchants have a higher possibility of fraudulent transactions?
    • H1: Some types of merchants are more likely to have fraudulent transactions than others
    • H0: The type of merchant does not affect the likelihood of a transaction being fraudulent
    • X: Merchant type (category)
    • Y: Fraud occurrence (is_fraud)

  2. RQ2: Would merchants in more populated cities be more likely to be fraudulent compared to those in less populated cities?
    • H1: Merchants in cities with larger populations are more likely to have fraudulent transactions than those in smaller cities
    • H0: The population size of a city does not affect how likely a merchant is to have fraudulent transactions
    • X: Number of population (city_pop)
    • Y: Fraud occurrence (is_fraud)

  3. RQ3: Would the transaction hour of the day affect the probability of fraud in digital transactions?
    • H1: Certain transaction hours of the day are more likely to have fraudulent transactions than others
    • H0: The transaction hour of the day does not affect the likelihood of a transaction being fraudulent
    • X: Transaction time (transaction_hour)
    • Y: Fraud occurrence (is_fraud)

Analytical Techniques

  • Clustering (k-means & model-based)
  • Predictive models (logistic regression, random forest, neural network, and deep learning)

Goal for Business Use Case

  • Minimize cost of loss, strengthen customer trust, and protect customers
  • Identify the most effective method to detect fraudulent transactions
    • Top priority is to identify as many real fraud cases as possible
    • Need high sensitivity and low false negative rate
    • Lower precision rate as a tradeoff is acceptable
